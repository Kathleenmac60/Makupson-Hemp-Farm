Industrial Hemp in Mississippi

Understanding hemp regulations, THC limits, and quality standards is critical for processors and manufacturers. This page provides clear, compliant information about industrial hemp and how Makupson Farming LLC approaches production.

Hemp FAQ

What is industrial hemp?

Industrial hemp is a regulated variety of the Cannabis sativa plant that contains no more than 0.3% Delta-9 THC on a dry-weight basis. Hemp is federally legal and is cultivated for commercial uses including CBD extraction, wellness products, and manufacturing inputs.

Is hemp legal in Mississippi?

Yes. Hemp cultivation is legal in Mississippi under state and federal law when growers are properly licensed and comply with THC testing requirements. Makupson Farming LLC operates in accordance with Mississippi hemp regulations.

How is THC compliance verified?

THC compliance is verified through required sampling and third-party laboratory testing. Crops must test at or below the legal THC threshold to be considered compliant industrial hemp.

What happens if hemp exceeds THC limits?

Hemp that tests above allowable THC limits is considered non-compliant and must be handled according to regulatory requirements. Indoor cultivation helps reduce variability that can lead to THC compliance risk.

Why does indoor cultivation matter for hemp?

Indoor cultivation allows for precise control of environmental conditions, resulting in more consistent cannabinoid profiles, lower contamination risk, and predictable harvest cycles compared to outdoor production.

Does Makupson Farming LLC make consumer CBD products?

No. Makupson Farming LLC operates as a B2B supplier, providing raw hemp materials to licensed processors and manufacturers rather than selling consumer products.
